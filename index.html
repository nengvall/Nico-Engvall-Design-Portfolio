Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

In our highly digitzed modern world, mass customization and consumerism are of the highest importance when it comes to the act of making. That being said, the role of rhetoric, or the art of persuasion, becomes very important in the realm of digital design and writing. The preferences, opinions, and physcological tendencies of viewers are to be always kept in mind in, and technology today can help us achieve wide viewer acceptance. As makers, we must keep this in mind if we are to make an impact in the world of design.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

There is a very simple reason that great designers and professional writers stick to basic principles: they work. It has been proven through countless case studies that design principles such as hierarchy, alignment, proximity, proportion, and color all have tremendous effects on how viewers interpret and interact with design peices. If done correctly and creatively, leveraging design principles harmoniously can elevate our design peices from simple personal work to exciting work that people will want to see. It is also important to keep the element of risk in mind when we are creating, as an extra personal flare can be a true difference maker.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

As life goes on, we will be working with other people in almost every aspect of our personal and professional lives. Engaging with others in a workshop environment can be extremely beneficial as it is next to impossible to improve our design peices without insight from another set of eyes. In the grand scheme of things, as young desginers we should never show our work just to ourselves and call it a day. We must be open to engaging with others by both asking for feedback and giving honest, constructive feedback to our peers. Whether we agree or not, we must learn to hear uncomfortable truths to improve our skills.

4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Once we gain feedback from a workshop environment, engaging in revision should be next. Just like anything we do in the world, the act of improving is a process. It can be a long process or short process, but taking the neccessary steps to learn how to revise our work through any medium avaiable to us is a priceless skill to have. Not only will it simply help improve our work, it shows we as designers obtain the principles of determination and receptiveness. There are countless tools we can use, but real effort is the only thing that can bring us to the light we are looking for when designing.

5. Learn to present your work.

Presenting our work to a group of people is very important because it helps bridge the gap between us and our personal fears. People are very open and receptive to people that talk with confidence and transparency, so naturally learning to present ourselves in the same way is a great skill to have. I feel learning the princples of presenting work is very easy, as it mostly pertains to body language, voice level, and the speed at which you present. The rest should contain who one is a person. We should present our true selfs and beliefs, yet we learn to do it as confidently as possible in order to be taken seriously, and then gain good feedback.
